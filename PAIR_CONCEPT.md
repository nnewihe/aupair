## The Problem

### Every Tool Stops at the Match. The Hard Part Starts After.

Matching platforms (Cultural Care, AuPairCare, AuPairWorld) have been well-funded and well-built, but the moment the match is made, families are entirely on their own. What follows is a year-long management relationship combining:

- **Employment** — scheduling, compliance, stipend calculation
- **Live-in cohabitation** — shared space, household rules, cultural differences
- **Childcare oversight** — routines, discipline, developmental needs
- **Cultural exchange** — integration, language, mutual growth
- **Personal mentorship** — feedback, support, emotional navigation

...all at once, with someone who may have never managed anything at all.

> "Families must tell an au pair exactly what they want — not assume she knows when it's time to change the baby, etc." — Berkeley Parents Network

The failures that result are not relationship failures. They are **management failures** — born of unclear expectations, undocumented hours, no framework for feedback, and no tools designed for this relationship. They are entirely preventable.

## What Pair Is

**Pair** is a five-phase SaaS platform built around the complete lifecycle of a host family / au pair relationship — from the first interview through the final handover. Every module is grounded in real templates built by real host families.

### The Five Phases

| Phase | Name | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Pre-Match | Interview toolkit, candidate evaluation, family goals |
| 1 | Onboarding | Family brief, household expectations, child profiles, Week 1 plan |
| 2 | Year Management | Time tracking, scheduling, compliance, summer planning |
| 3 | Performance | Quarterly reviews, check-ins, feedback, relationship health |
| 4 | Transition | Off-boarding, knowledge transfer, handover, retrospective |

### Evidence Base

The feature set is not speculative. It is derived from real artefacts built by the founding team:
- A structured interview template (11 sections)
- A detailed host family guidelines document (8 sections including per-child profiles and meal plans)
- A live timesheet (August 2025–present, with split shifts and overtime calculations)
- A summer schedule planner (multi-person, hour-by-hour)
- A weighted quarterly performance review with a five-level rating rubric and per-criteria definitions

## Phase 0: Pre-Match — Interview & Candidate Evaluation

The interview is where the relationship begins — and where most families are most underprepared. Pair provides a structured, guided interview toolkit that captures what matters, surfaces red flags, and seeds the onboarding documents that follow.

**Key features:**
- **Structured Interview Guide** — 11-section guided flow covering childcare experience, emergency handling, discipline philosophy, daily routine management, adaptability, cultural exchange readiness, driving, expectations and boundaries, special needs experience, homesickness and adjustment, and long-term commitment
- **Candidate Answer Capture** — structured fields per section; creates a lasting record of what was discussed and agreed before arrival
- **Special Needs Flag** — expands into relevant experience, behaviour plan familiarity, therapist coordination, sensory management; feeds directly into child profile
- **Candidate Question Capture → Household FAQ** — the au pair's own interview questions become the seed of the Household FAQ, answered by the family pre-arrival
- **Comparison & Shortlist** — compare multiple candidates across key criteria; rate and annotate; export a clean shortlist
- **Family Goals Wizard** — prompts families to articulate priorities: childcare style, cultural exchange goals, household expectations, communication preferences; generates a shareable "family brief"
- **Discipline Philosophy Builder** — families document their approach (prior notice, time-boxing, active dialogue, processing space, choice, consequences, hard rules); feeds into performance review criteria and visible to the au pair from day one

## Phase 1: Onboarding — Family Brief, Expectations & Week 1

The onboarding module generates a complete, personalised family brief — the document that currently takes experienced host families hours to write from scratch and that most families never produce at all. For the incoming au pair, it is their field manual. For the family, it is their single source of truth.

### Goals & Philosophy
- **Family Goals Wizard** — articulate priorities; generate a shareable pre-arrival brief
- **Family Welcome Presentation Builder** — guided visual presentation: who we are, how we live, what success looks like
- **Discipline Philosophy Builder** — documented approach; feeds into performance reviews; visible from day one

### Responsibilities & Household Rules
- **Primary & Additional Responsibilities Template** — childcare duties, school pick-up, laundry, food preparation and clean-up, bedroom management, seasonal organisation projects
- **Housemate Expectations Section** — meals, kitchen clean-up, room and bathroom standards, common areas, curfew, visitor policy, overnight guest rules
- **General Household Info Builder** — appliance instructions, HVAC and thermostat guidance, safety systems; captured once, never re-explained
- **Safety Rules Generator** — family-specific document covering: no unattended children, kitchen safety, door/window rules, medication handling, driving rules, emergency protocols; generates a clean PDF
- **Screen Time & Media Philosophy** — TV, tablets, and apps: daily limits, approved content, specific restrictions per child; prevents silent divergence on a major friction source

### Benefits, Car & Travel
- **Benefits Package Builder** — phone plan, gym, streaming, transportation allowance, family-specific perks; a clear written record of what was offered and agreed
- **Car Sharing Guidelines** — insurance coverage, geographic restrictions, curfew for car use, overnight rules, incident protocols, EV charging responsibilities, pre-license transition plan
- **Travel Expectations Module** — working vs. non-working time during trips, room arrangements, duty hours while travelling, international travel protocol
- **Au Pair Vacation Policy** — mandatory J-1 two-week paid vacation, notice requirements, blackout dates, split vacation rules, request process
- **Bonus & Recognition Philosophy** — what triggers a bonus, what "Outstanding" unlocks, how exceptional contributions are acknowledged; sets mutual expectations upfront

### Child Profiles & Meal Planning
- **Per-Child Profile Builder** — dietary needs, communication style, emotional regulation, preferred activities, learning goals, potty status, social development notes, medical/allergy info, school schedule and pick-up
- **Meal Plan Builder** — weekly grid (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks) with per-child dietary flags, alternate options, preparation notes; configurable for school days vs. home days
- **Weekly Household Task Template** — day-by-day recurring duty assignment; auto-populates the schedule module

### Arrival Preparation
- **Household FAQ — "No Silly Questions"** — a living FAQ seeded by the family and added to by the au pair; questions that go unasked because they feel too small, captured once and always available
- **Pre-Arrival Checklist** — "Here's How We Run Things" covering house rules, children's routines, emergency contacts, and household expectations
- **Week 1 Plan Template** — day-by-day structured landing plan with rationale annotations

## Phase 2: Year Management — Time, Planning & Compliance

Families are running sophisticated weekly calculations in Google Sheets — split shifts, planned vs. actual hours, stipend math, overtime balances, school calendar notes — entirely by hand. Pair replaces this with a purpose-built system.

### Hours Tracking & Compliance
- **Split-Shift Daily Logger** — two start/end time pairs per day; supports inline day notes; both parties give weekly acknowledgement
- **Planned vs. Actual Hours** — set planned hours at week start; flag divergences before they become disputes
- **Automatic Stipend Calculation** — weekly pay auto-calculated from logged hours against J-1 minimum ($195.75 baseline); overtime tracked separately; running total visible to both parties
- **J-1 Compliance Guardrails** — real-time alerts approaching the 45-hour weekly / 10-hour daily cap; soft warning at 40 hours, hard alert at 44
- **State-Specific Compliance Engine** — state selection mandatory at setup; California, Massachusetts, New York, Washington, and Illinois treat au pairs as domestic employees under state law; stipend calculator automatically applies correct wage floor and overtime thresholds; families who relocate mid-placement can update state and system recalculates proactively
- **Sick, Vacation & Other Hours Tracking** — separate columns per category; running vacation balance vs. J-1 entitlement

### Calendar & Year Planning
- **Year-View Calendar with School Integration** — "No School," "Early Release Day," and "Federal Holiday" as first-class colour-coded event types; US school districts pre-loaded for top 50 markets
- **Typical Week Templates** — standard weekly schedule auto-populates future weeks; school-year and summer templates maintained separately
- **Holiday & Break Planner** — identifies four to six major scheduling pressure points (Thanksgiving, winter break, spring break, summer); proactive reminders 3–4 weeks ahead
- **Summer Planner Mode** — activated when school year ends; structured day with named activity blocks; anchor activity scheduling; multi-person coordination; caregiver handover log

### Communications & Alignment
- **Communications Log** — a neutral, shared record of agreements, schedule changes, and important conversations; not a chat tool; timestamped, searchable, exportable

## Phase 3: Performance Management — Feedback, Check-ins & Growth

The founding team's quarterly performance review — weighted categories, two-column feedback format, five-level rating scale with per-criterion definitions, personal closing note — is the gold standard for what this module produces.

### Structured Review System
- **Weighted Category Review Template** — four configurable categories with family-set weights (e.g., Overall Childcare 30%, Child Engagement 40%, Household Membership 10%, Personal Growth 20%); sub-criteria drawn from child profiles and discipline philosophy set in onboarding
- **Two-Column Feedback Format** — "What's Working Well / Growth Areas" per sub-category; guided prompts prevent vague generalities
- **Five-Level Rating Scale with Definitions** — Weak → Improvement Required → Good → Great → Outstanding; each level has a specific behavioural definition per sub-category
- **Baseline Comparison** — prior period rating carried forward; progress visible at a glance; narrative of development over time
- **Personal Closing Note** — guided prompt for a warm closing message; contextualises structured feedback
- **Bonus & Recognition Trigger** — on review completion, system checks against the bonus philosophy set in onboarding; if "Outstanding" criteria met, family is prompted to action the recognition they committed to upfront

### Ongoing Check-ins & Relationship Health
- **Monthly / Quarterly Check-in Templates** — lighter-touch guided conversation guides between formal reviews
- **Two-Way Feedback System** — both parties can give and receive feedback; normalises the au pair's voice
- **Relationship Health Pulse** — monthly 2–3 question private sentiment check-in for the family; surfaces patterns; prompts a conversation before a small issue becomes a big one
- **Difficult Conversation Guides** — situation-specific templates for conversations families dread: hygiene, phone use with children, cultural differences around food or bedtime, performance issues

### Au Pair Support Section (always free)
- **How to Receive Feedback** — guide for au pairs on hearing and processing feedback constructively
- **How to Give Feedback to Your Host Family** — templates for raising concerns, requesting changes, flagging issues respectfully
- **How to Ask for What You Need** — practical guides for common au pair needs that go unasked because they feel too small or too awkward
- **Programme Rights Reference** — plain-language summary of J-1 rights: maximum hours, minimum stipend, mandatory vacation, educational requirements, what to do if these are not being honoured

## Phase 4: Transition — Off-boarding, Handover & Continuity

Most families host 2–3 au pairs over their childcare years. A well-managed transition creates a positive ending for the departing au pair, dramatically reduces ramp-up time for the incoming one, and protects continuity of care for the children.

- **60-Day Transition Countdown** — begins the process 60 days out: conversation with the au pair, administrative tasks (visa extension check, final stipend calculation), child preparation
- **Knowledge Transfer Document** — built collaboratively with the departing au pair; structured around child profiles from onboarding; automatically formatted as a briefing for the incoming au pair
- **Caregiver-to-Caregiver Handover Session** — guided prompts for a structured briefing from departing to incoming caregiver; covering each child's current state, relationship dynamics, unwritten household rules, and practical tips that never make it into documents
- **Summer-to-Fall Transition Mode** — mid-year transitions handled with the same structured handover flow as end-of-year transitions
- **Year-in-Review Auto-Summary** — auto-generated from logged data: total hours worked, goals achieved, performance trajectory, milestones; a meaningful gift to the departing au pair
- **Reference Letter Builder** — guided prompts drawing from performance review history; makes it easy to give a genuine, specific professional endorsement
- **Family Retrospective** — what worked, what to do differently, what to look for next time; surfaces as context when the family begins the interview process again

## Revenue Model

### Three Vectors

**B2C Subscription** — host families pay $9–$22/month. Au pairs access the support section free — driving word-of-mouth within tightly-networked communities. Freemium-to-paid conversion is the primary growth engine in Years 1–2.

**Agency Revenue Share** — au pair agencies direct families to Pair and receive 30–40% of subscription revenue in Year 1, stepping down to 20% in Year 2 and 10% in Year 3. The family's relationship is with Pair directly — full product, full brand, full data. Agencies earn passive recurring income for a warm referral. White-label considered only if required as a condition of a large-scale deal.

**International Expansion** — the US programme structure has close parallels in the UK, Germany, France, and the Netherlands. These markets collectively exceed the US installed base. Expansion is a Phase 3 initiative with country-specific compliance built into the architecture from day one.

## V1 Product Roadmap

### Phase 0: Foundation & Validation (Months 1–2)
Validate assumptions before building. Recruit 10–15 host families for structured interviews. Confirm willingness to pay, rank feature priorities, finalise technical stack.

**Activities:**
- 10–15 host family interviews; pain point ranking and pricing validation
- PairTrack deep-dive: full feature audit; partnership/acquisition conversation
- Technical architecture: stack decision; school calendar data source; auth model
- Partner recruitment: technical co-founder; design partner; 1–2 advisors

### Phase 1: MVP — Onboarding + Core Time Tracking (Months 3–6)
Ship the two most differentiated features first: the onboarding toolkit (no competitor has this) and J-1-compliant time tracking meaningfully better than PairTrack.

**Features:**
- Goals Wizard + Family Brief: full onboarding document generator; shareable pre-arrival
- Child Profile Builder: dietary, emotional, learning, medical fields
- Week 1 Plan + Pre-Arrival Checklist
- Household FAQ: family-seeded; au pair-addable
- Split-Shift Hours Logger: two time pairs/day; planned vs. actual; inline notes
- Stipend Calculator: auto-calculation; overtime/additional balance; weekly acknowledgement
- School Calendar Integration: top 20 US districts; No School / Early Release / Federal Holiday types
- Compliance Alerts: 45hr weekly / 10hr daily guardrails
- Communications Log: shared neutral record; timestamped; exportable
- Mobile-Responsive Web App: iOS + Android browser-compatible

Add the performance management layer, complete the planning module, launch au pair support section. Begin converting beta users to paid subscriptions.
